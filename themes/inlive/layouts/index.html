What is inLive

To help you develop a new live streaming experience.

We are a live streaming infrastructure as a service. Save your time to focus on the live streaming use case, let us handle the complicated server setup, streaming cost optimization, video hosting infrastructure, and all the complex technical requirements you need to develop a live streaming app.

What we offer

Self-serving live streaming infrastructure.

No upfront commitment, no meeting or call needed. Just use our examples, documentation, and tutorials to build your own live streaming application. We focus on developer experiences to remove all barrier entries for the developer to develop their next live streaming application.

  • REST API to create and broadcast live streaming
  • JavaScript SDK for easier development
  • WebRTC and RTMP (coming soon) streaming input

Why us

Issue with live streaming cost and how it's not affordable for developing countries.

Live streaming is awesome and has become an essential part of online communication and entertainment, but building live streaming features on your own can be super hard.

Users in developing countries often have different live streaming use cases that are not too high on usage compared to other common use cases. This comparison have significant impact on cost in the long run and needs cost-effective solutions.

We understand that cost is the most important consideration and can be a major barrier for many businesses and individuals getting into live streaming, especially in developing countries.

That's why we are committed to provide reliable live streaming service with competitive pricing to be affordable based on purchasing power parity, which means that we adjust our prices to take into account the cost of living in developing countries.

With our service, you can get started with live streaming to reach a wider audience without worrying about the overpriced cost.

Comparison pricing between live streaming vendors (Agora, Cloudflare, Amazon, inLive).

For example, if using Agora, Cloudflare, or Amazon Web Services for live streaming in 720p quality, we can expect data transfer in GB to be around 0.1-0.2 GB per minute and cost for data transfer to be around $0.015-0.030 per GB. In 1 hour streaming and watched by 100 viewers, total data transfer to be around 600-1200GB and total cost to be around $9-36. That cost is not affordable in developing countries if in some conditions users want to stream 10 hours per month with viewers higher than 100.

Our Mission

Provide an affordable live streaming service.

Most live streaming services use minutes of views as their price unit. We're doing things differently. If they charge you $xx per minute view, they are charging you for the highest video resolution and bandwidth cost. But in reality, in developing countries where most people use cheap phones with low-quality screens, they'll watch lower-resolution videos to save on internet data. That's why it's fair to charge our service using bandwidth usage. This way, you only pay for what you use.

Our mission to provide an affordable live-streaming service has not ended yet. We are working to make it even more affordable by using modern video codecs to reduce bandwidth usage to lower the cost per viewer. In the long run, we're aiming to build our own video CDN to make it even more cost-effective. So you can get a more affordable live-streaming service.

Our secret weapon? Affordable but reliable CDN, fair pricing unit, and on-demand live streaming server. Most of the live streaming services use expensive CDN, which makes their prices high. These expensive CDNs are meant for big events like sports games that are watched by hundreds of thousands or even millions of viewers. But most live streaming apps for creators only have a few hundred viewers, so we don't need that expensive CDN. That's why we use an affordable CDN but reliable enough to handle your live streaming needs.

How to get started

Here's a quick guide to getting started.

  1. 1

    Sign up for an account

    First things first, you'll need to create an account by clicking "Login" in the menu. You'll be prompted to choose between Google or GitHub to log in. Once you've completed this step, you'll be able to log in to your account.

  2. 2

    Set up your live stream API key

    Once you've logged in, you'll be taken to your dashboard. We call it "Studio". From there, you can click "Settings" in the side menu to set up your live stream API key.

  3. 3

    Develop your live stream

    To start streaming, you'll need to develop your live streaming features in your application using our SDK library or essential kit. You can find detailed instructions and sample code on our developer documentation page. The documentation page also includes guides for integration, troubleshooting, and other development-related topics.

  4. 4

    Start streaming

    Once you've developed a live stream in your application, you're ready to start streaming!

We hope this guide has been helpful in getting you started to implement your live streaming with our service. If you have any questions or need further assistance, please don't hesitate to reach out to our support team. We're always here to help!
